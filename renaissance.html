Renaissance Art

The Renaissance marked a remarkable rebirth of art, knowledge, and creativity in Europe between the 14th and 17th centuries. Artists like Leonardo da Vinci, Michelangelo, and Raphael revolutionized painting and sculpture by introducing techniques such as perspective, realism, and anatomical accuracy. Their works portrayed human emotions, scientific curiosity, and beauty with unmatched depth. The period blended art with science—Leonardo dissected bodies to perfect his sketches, while architects like Brunelleschi introduced mathematical precision in buildings. Renaissance art symbolized a shift toward humanism, celebrating human potential rather than focusing solely on religious themes. This cultural movement transformed Europe intellectually and artistically, influencing modern art, design, and scientific exploration. Its masterpieces continue to inspire creativity and innovation worldwide.
