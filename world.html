World Wars

The World Wars were two of the most transformative and devastating events in global history. World War I began in 1914, fueled by nationalism, military alliances, and political tensions. The trench warfare, massive casualties, and new weapons like tanks and poison gas changed the nature of conflict forever. Just two decades later, World War II erupted due to unresolved tensions, the rise of dictatorships, and global economic struggles. This war introduced the world to large-scale air combat, naval battles, and the atomic bomb. Beyond destruction, the wars reshaped world politics, leading to the United Nations, the Cold War, and new superpowers. They accelerated advancements in technology, medicine, and civil rights movements while reminding humanity of the cost of aggression and intolerance. Understanding these wars helps us recognize the importance of peace, diplomacy, and global cooperation.
